Fergus, a prominent hero in Irish mythology, boasts a name that epitomizes masculinity, translating to "male potency" or "manhood" (sometimes even as "semen"). There are a myriad of characters that bear this name, including 10 members of the renowned group of warriors known as the Fianna. Given the large number of individuals with this name, some have proposed that Fergus may not have been a proper name but rather a title of kingship.
