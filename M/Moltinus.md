``` tab [group1:Info]
::: magazinestyle
Continental Celtic mythology includes Moltinus, a deity whose name shares etymology with modern words for sheep, such as "mutton" in English and "mouton" in French. Scholars suggest that the name may signify "ram." Historical records only offer two inscriptions referencing this deity, who was not assimilated by the Romans with their own gods, in contrast to many other Celtic divinities.

:::
```
