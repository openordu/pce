Étain, also known as Etaine, Aideen, Édain, Edain or Achtan, is a heroine or goddess from Irish mythology. It can be difficult to distinguish between the various mythological figures who bear her name, as their stories are often intertwined with themes of reincarnation, disguise, and mistaken identity. It's possible that they all ultimately stem from a single source, potentially a sun goddess. Her name means "swift one" and the early epithet Echraide, which means "horse-rider", connects her with equine goddesses like the Welsh Rhiannon, the continental Celtic Epona, and the Irish Macha.
