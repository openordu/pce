::: magazinestyle
A notable figure in Irish tales was Guaire, known for his great generosity. He was the father of Ébhlinne, a goddess from Munster who lived far away from Guaire's home in the Brú Na Bóinne, a prehistoric stone complex located in the north near River Boyne. Additionally, another important Guaire was an actual king of CONNaCHT. He was known for providing meals to anchorites located in the nearby mountains by having food transported through the air. Currently, one can still visit Guaire's castle in Kinvarra in Co. Galway. This castle was where Guaire issued a challenge to the Bard Senchán, leading to the return of the lost epic, Táin Bó Cuailnge, from the Otherworld.

:::
