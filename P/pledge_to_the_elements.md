``` tab [group1:Info]
::: magazinestyle
Continental Celtic ritual involves a vow to the elements. A similar oath was taken in Gaul, which went as follows: “Should I betray my promise, may the skies collapse over me, may the seas submerge me, may the earth engulf me.” This pledge invokes the air, sea, and earth to testify to the speaker’s sincerity. The phrase reflects the Celtic belief in Animism, where the environment is considered sentient and, in this situation, vigilant.

:::
```
